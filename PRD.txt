Two proper product approaches

You asked for two cases. Here’s the correct split.

Case A — Policy-first engine

This is the easiest, fastest, and safest hackathon route.

How it works

You fetch:

privacy policy
terms of service
safety / child policy pages
cookie policy
FAQ / support docs if relevant

Then run:

section extraction
data category detection
third-party sharing detection
minors / child data flags
retention / deletion / consent / opt-out detection
readability scoring
risk scoring
Pros
easiest to demo
most realistic for hackathon
fast MVP
no device sandboxing needed
looks polished and practical
Cons
depends on the truthfulness of the policy
cannot detect hidden behavior directly
Best use

Start with this first.

Case B — Behavior-first engine

This is the more advanced and powerful route.

How it works

Instead of only trusting the policy, you analyze:

permissions requested
trackers / SDK hints
network requests
endpoint domains
analytics / ads / third-party patterns
mismatch between policy claims and behavior
Pros
much stronger product vision
more defensible
judges will find it smarter
can expose hidden issues
Cons
harder to implement fully in a hackathon
requires controlled testing / instrumentation
more moving parts
Best use

Show this as:

a partial working prototype
or a clear roadmap / future scope
or a second mode called “Deep Audit”
My recommendation

For hackathon:

Build this hybrid version
MVP

Policy-first fully working
+

Deep Audit

Behavior-first partially simulated or semi-working

That gives you:

a clean real product
a believable roadmap
stronger pitch
